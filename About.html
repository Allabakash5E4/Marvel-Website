CAPTAIN AMERICA

Captain America is a fictional superhero appearing in American comic books published by Marvel Comics. The character was created by writer Joe Simon and artist Jack Kirby and first appeared in "Captain America Comics" #1 in March 1941. Captain America is one of the most iconic and enduring characters in the Marvel Universe.Captain America is a beloved character who has played a significant role in the Marvel Universe for decades, representing the ideals of heroism, courage, and sacrifice. His stories often delve into themes of duty, identity, and the consequences of war, making him a complex and enduring figure in popular culture.

IRON MAN

Iron Man is a fictional superhero character who appears in American comic books published by Marvel Comics. Created by writer and editor Stan Lee, scripter Larry Lieber, artist Don Heck, and designed by artist Jack Kirby, Iron Man made his first appearance in "Tales of Suspense" #39 in 1963. The character is known for his high-tech armored suit, genius-level intellect, and alter ego, Tony Stark.Iron Man is a character known for his wit, charm, and technological prowess. He represents the fusion of advanced technology with the superhero genre, and his storylines often explore themes of responsibility, redemption, and the consequences of unchecked power. Iron Man remains a beloved and enduring figure in the world of comics and pop culture.











THOR

Thor is a fictional superhero appearing in American comic books published by Marvel Comics. The character was created by writer Stan Lee, scripter Larry Lieber, and artist Jack Kirby. Thor made his first appearance in "Journey into Mystery" #83 in 1962. Thor is a prominent member of the Marvel Universe and is inspired by the Norse god of thunder and lightning, also named Thor.Thor is known for his noble and heroic personality, his love of battle, and his sense of honor. He represents a fusion of ancient mythology with the superhero genre, and his stories often explore themes of duty, destiny, and the clash between mortal and divine realms.

HULK

The Hulk is a fictional superhero appearing in American comic books published by Marvel Comics. Created by writer Stan Lee and artist Jack Kirby, the character first appeared in "The Incredible Hulk" #1 in 1962. The Hulk is one of Marvel's most iconic and enduring characters.The Hulk is a complex character whose stories often explore themes of identity, anger, and the consequences of unchecked power. He is a unique superhero in that he is both a hero and a potential threat, depending on his emotional state. The character has been a fan favorite for decades and continues to be a prominent figure in the world of comics and pop culture.

BLACK WIDOW

Black Widow is a fictional superheroine and spy who appears in American comic books published by Marvel Comics. Created by writer Stan Lee, scripter Don Rico, and artist Don Heck, Black Widow made her first appearance in "Tales of Suspense" #52 in 1964. She is known for her exceptional combat skills, espionage expertise, and association with various superhero teams, including the Avengers.Black Widow is a character known for her resilience, resourcefulness, and ability to navigate the complex world of espionage and superheroics. Her stories often involve themes of redemption, identity, and the consequences of one's past actions. She is a beloved and enduring figure in the world of comics and has gained even greater recognition and popularity through her appearances in the Marvel Cinematic Universe.


CLINT

Clint Barton, also known as Hawkeye, is a fictional superhero appearing in American comic books published by Marvel Comics. Created by writer Stan Lee and artist Don Heck, Hawkeye made his first appearance in "Tales of Suspense" #57 in 1964. He is known for his exceptional archery skills, marksmanship, and association with various superhero teams, including the Avengers.Hawkeye is a character known for his determination, resourcefulness, and loyalty to his friends and teammates. Despite lacking superhuman abilities, he has proven himself to be a formidable hero through his skills, wit, and determination to make a difference in the superhero world.

SPIDER-MAN

Spider-Man, also known as Peter Parker, is one of Marvel Comics' most iconic and enduring superheroes. He was created by writer Stan Lee and artist Steve Ditko and made his first appearance in "Amazing Fantasy" #15 in 1962. Spider-Man's character is an integral part of the Marvel Universe and has played a significant role in the company's comic book history. Spider-Man is not only a beloved superhero but also a symbol of relatability and perseverance. His enduring popularity can be attributed to his compelling character development, memorable stories, and the way he resonates with audiences of all ages.





BLACK PANTHER

Black Panther is a prominent superhero character in the Marvel Comics universe. Created by writer Stan Lee and artist Jack Kirby, Black Panther, whose real name is T'Challa, made his first appearance in "Fantastic Four" #52 in 1966. He holds the distinction of being the first black superhero in mainstream comic books.Black Panther remains a beloved and influential character in both the comic book world and popular culture at large. His unique blend of superheroics, African heritage, and leadership qualities have made him an enduring and iconic figure in the Marvel Universe.


WAR MACHINE

In the Marvel Comics universe, James "Rhodey" Rhodes, commonly known as War Machine, is a fictional superhero character who first appeared in "Iron Man" #118 in 1979. Created by writer David Michelinie and artist Bob Layton, Rhodey has become a significant supporting character in the Iron Man and larger Marvel Universe.War Machine, as portrayed by James "Rhodey" Rhodes, is a character known for his military discipline, technological expertise, and his contributions to the superhero community. His journey from being a close ally of Iron Man to adopting his own superhero persona as War Machine has made him a memorable figure in Marvel Comics and the MCU.






ANT-MAN

Ant-Man is a superhero character in the Marvel Comics universe. The character has been portrayed by several individuals, but the most well-known is Dr. Henry "Hank" Pym, who first appeared in "Tales to Astonish" #27 in 1962. Ant-Man's unique ability is to shrink down to the size of an ant while retaining his full human strength.Ant-Man is a character known for his ability to change size, his scientific expertise, and his contributions to the Marvel Universe. His adventures often blend science fiction with superhero action, resulting in unique and entertaining stories.


DOCTOR STRANGE

Doctor Strange, whose real name is Dr. Stephen Vincent Strange, is a fictional character in the Marvel Comics universe. He was created by writer Stan Lee and artist Steve Ditko and first appeared in "Strange Tales" #110 in 1963. Doctor Strange is often referred to as the Sorcerer Supreme, a title he holds as the most powerful and skilled practitioner of the mystic arts in the Marvel Universe.Doctor Strange is a unique and compelling character in the Marvel Universe, known for his complex personality, mastery of magic, and his role as a defender of the mystical and supernatural aspects of the Marvel Universe. His stories often blend elements of superhero action with the mystical and metaphysical, making him a fascinating and enduring character in the comic book world.






THE FALCON & THE WINTER SOLDIER

Sam Wilson, as the Falcon, is a beloved character in the Marvel Universe, known for his heroic qualities, his unique powers, and his role as a symbol of hope and justice. His journey from a military veteran to a superhero and the new Captain America is a central theme in the ongoing MCU narrative.Bucky Barnes, also known as the Winter Soldier, is a fictional character in the Marvel Comics universe. He was created by Joe Simon and Jack Kirby and first appeared in "Captain America Comics" #1 in 1941. In the comics, Bucky Barnes was initially portrayed as the teenage sidekick to Captain America during World War II.


THE WANDA & THE VISION

Wanda Maximoff (Scarlet Witch) and Vision are two prominent characters in the Marvel Comics universe who have also been featured prominently in the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU). Wanda Maximoff and Vision are complex characters with rich backstories and distinct powers. Their romantic relationship and the challenges they face as individuals and as a couple have been central to their character development in both the comics and the MCU, making them integral parts of the Marvel Universe.







SHANG-CHI

Shang-Chi, also known as the Master of Kung Fu, is a fictional character in the Marvel Comics universe. He made his first appearance in "Special Marvel Edition" #15 in 1973. Shang-Chi is a skilled martial artist and has been a prominent character in Marvel Comics for decades. Shang-Chi's character represents not only a master of martial arts but also a symbol of Asian representation in popular culture. His introduction to the MCU expands the diversity of superhero characters and brings martial arts to the forefront of the Marvel cinematic universe.
